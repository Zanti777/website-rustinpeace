EDITION 2
'Eco Chamber'

BUY

The winter storms often bring treasures washed ashore near my home, like an old rusted gas cylinder found rolling in the tidal flow. On close inspection, this 'junk' had become a new home to some on its long journey through the seas. Crustaceans, barnacles, seaweed, and other organisms had taken up residence on its corroded metal surface, forming a miniature ecosystem that was coexisting with the cylinder's decay. The juxtaposition of the manufactured object hosting new life left me with an appreciation for the resilience of the natural world, which is echoed in this piece "Eco Chamber." The artwork depicts the transformation of objects that harbour toxic substances into something where life can thrive and invites us to nurture and support the growth of the natural world rather than bring harm upon it.

The title, "Masquerade," alludes to the masking or disguising of one's true identity. It evokes a sense of obscurity and distortion.
